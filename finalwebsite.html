Hi! I'm Christine B. Manabat, a first-year Bachelor of Science in Information Technology student at Nueva Ecija University of Science and Technology.My experience being a student can be a time of growth and self-discovery, but for me, it has also involved silent struggles. Balancing academic pressure with personal challenges, I often faced financial difficulties that made it hard to concentrate, sometimes studying on an empty stomach. Home life added to the stress, and managing my mental health became an ongoing battle. Despite moments of wanting to give up and feeling lost about the future, I found strength in the dream of a better life, the support of friends, and my own resilience. Each exam passed and project completed felt like a small victory. My experiences taught me important lessons about survival, adaptability, and perseverance, which have proven more valuable than perfect grades as long as you didn't fail.

Goals as a Student and a Daughter

My goal as a student is to learn, to grow, and to never take this opportunity for granted. But my goal as a daughter is to lift the weight they carried for so long—to be the reason their sacrifices were worth it. Yes, the journey is hard. There are moments I feel tired, unsure, even lost. But when I think of my parents, I remember why I started. I’m not just doing this for me. I’m doing it for them for every time they believed in me when I couldn’t believe in myself. So I keep going. Because my dream isn’t just mine, it's theirs too.
